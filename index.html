About Safe Communities International (SCI)

Safe Communities International (SCI) is a non-profit, community-focused organization established to promote safety, health, security, and resilience among communities and institutions. SCI operates on the principle that preventable injuries, occupational hazards, and disaster-related risks can be significantly reduced through coordinated, evidence-based, and community-driven interventions.

Headquartered in Uganda and operating with an international outlook, SCI supports national and regional efforts aligned with global frameworks for public health, occupational safety, and disaster risk reduction. The organization’s work is consistent with the principles and guidance of the World Health Organization (WHO), the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), and internationally recognized best practices in safety and risk management.

SCI designs and implements programs in occupational safety and health (OSH), community safety, emergency preparedness, disaster risk reduction, and capacity development. These programs emphasize prevention, risk identification, preparedness, and resilience-building, with particular attention to vulnerable populations and high-risk environments.

The organization works in collaboration with government institutions, private sector actors, civil society organizations, and development partners to strengthen systems, build local capacity, and promote sustainable safety outcomes. SCI is committed to transparency, accountability, and measurable impact, ensuring that all interventions are responsive to local needs while meeting international standards.

Through its work, Safe Communities International contributes to safer workplaces, resilient communities, and strengthened institutional capacity, advancing the shared global objective of protecting life, health, and livelihoods across borders.

Our Mission

To promote safety, health, security, and resilience by supporting communities, institutions, and organizations through evidence-based interventions, capacity building, and collaborative partnerships that prevent harm and protect life.

Our Objectives

  • To reduce preventable injuries, occupational hazards, and safety-related risks in communities and workplaces.
  • To strengthen emergency preparedness, disaster risk reduction, and response capacity.
  • To provide professional safety training, risk assessments, and advisory services aligned with international standards.
  • To support government, private sector, and civil society initiatives that promote sustainable safety systems.
  • To contribute to national and international safety goals in line with WHO and UN frameworks.

Our Core Values

  • Integrity: We operate with honesty, transparency, and accountability.
  • Prevention: We prioritize proactive measures to prevent harm before it occurs.
  • Community-Centered Approach: We value local participation, ownership, and inclusiveness.
  • Professional Excellence: We uphold high technical and ethical standards in all our work.
  • Partnership: We collaborate across sectors to achieve sustainable impact.
